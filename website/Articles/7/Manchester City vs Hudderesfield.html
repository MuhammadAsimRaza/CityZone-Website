Manchester City vs Huddersfield Town, FA Cup Third-Round Preview:

Manchester City are due to begin their defence of the FA Cup in the third round vs Championship side Huddersfield Town.

The Blues enter the competition as holders thanks to a 2-1 win vs local nemesis Manchester United at Wembley Stadium, the second-leg of a historic Treble.

Fresh off being crowned Champions of the World, the Blues have gotten back to form domestically, securing a come-from-behind 1-3 win at Everton, before securing a 2-0 win vs basement-dwelling Sheffield United, following goals from Rodri and Julian Alvarez.

The Terriers come in sat in a relegation battle in the Championship. They're 21st in their division, and have won two of their last ten games.

Head-to-head

City hasn't lost to Huddersfield in this millennium. The last Terriers win was in November 1999, a 0-1 result at Maine Road. The Blues have won four of the last five meetings between the two.

Where to Watch

Viewers in the UK can watch this 14:00 GMT kick-off on the BBC Red Button.

Team News

Pep Guardiola's men have received three fitness boosts, as Jeremy Doku, Erling Haaland and Kevin De Bruyne are all back in full training, with De Bruyne a contender to get minutes following his appearance on the bench vs Sheffield United.

Huddersfield Town will be without Japanese international Yuta Nakayama as he departs for the Asian Cup.

Score Prediction

With more returnees to the Manchester City squad coupled with the match being at Etihad Stadium, a place City haven't suffered defeat for more than a year, it's difficult to see a Huddersfield victory.

Manchester City 4-0 Huddersfield Town.
