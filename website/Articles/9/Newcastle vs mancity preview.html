Newcastle United vs Manchester City Match Preview, Premier League

Manchester City look to get off to a winning start in Premier League play for 2024 as they travel to St. Jamesâ€™ Park on Saturday evening.

The Quintuple winners enter the weekend fresh off a 5-0 hammering of Huddersfield Town in the third round of the Emirates FA Cup, spearheaded by two Phil Foden goals, a Jeremy Doku goal, a Julian Alvarez goal and an assist on return for Kevin De Bruyne.

City have re-gained some consistency in the league of late; the Blues had only one win in six between the middle of November and December, but have won their last three domestic games - two of which in league play.

Newcastle United find themselves ninth in the Premier League table, something of a surprise given their fourth-placed finish and Champions League qualification last season. They've lost their last 3 games in the league (1-0, Luton [A], 1-3, Nottingham Forest [H], 4-2, Liverpool [A]), though they also advanced in the FA Cup at the expense of bitter rivals Sunderland (0-3) in the third round.

Head-to-head

City have only lost twice to the Magpies under the reign of Pep Guardiola, with one of those being a Premier League defeat (2-1, 29/01/2019; 1-0, 27/09/2023). The Blues have won 4 of the last 5 in the league against Newcastle.

Where to Watch

Newcastle United v Manchester City will kick-off at 17:30 UK time, and can be viewed on TNT Sports.

Team News

Pep Guardiola during his press conference confirmed that Erling Haaland and John Stones will not feature on Tyneside. He has hinted at Kevin De Bruyne making a return to the Starting XI.

Newcastle have a plethora of absentees. They have had a lot of injuries, leading to a lacklustre league campaign. Noteworthy absentees include Nick Pope, Joelinton, Callum Wilson, Sandro Tonali (suspended), etc.

Score Prediction

Manchester City have fewer absentees and even without them. Newcastle are still a good side, potentially good enough to cause some problems.
